Welcome to the **FAQs & Troubleshooting** section. Here, you’ll find answers to commonly asked questions, along with solutions for common issues you might encounter while using the Gateway. This section is designed to help you get the most out of our products and resolve issues quickly.

## **Frequently Asked Questions**

<Accordion title="Getting Started">
  <Accordion title="How do I create a Merchant Account?">
    Visit our [Create an Account](./guides/create-account) page for step-by-step instructions on setting up your merchant account.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What documents are needed to onboard as a merchant?">
    For a full list of required documents, see our [Onboarding Checklist](./guides/onboarding-checklist).
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How do I add products to my account?">
    Once your account is set up, navigate to the **Products** tab and select the products you want to enable. Each product guide provides further details.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What is MID?">
    A Payment MID (Merchant Identification Number) is a unique identifier assigned to a business by Payment Options. This number is used to track and authenticate transactions made by the merchant, ensuring that payments are processed and settled to the correct business account.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What is acquirer?">
    An acquirer (also called an acquiring bank, acquiring financial institution, or merchant bank) is a financial institution or payment processor that partners with merchants to enable them to accept credit card, debit card, and other electronic payments. The acquirer provides the merchant with a merchant account and facilitates the authorization, processing, and settlement of transactions between the merchant and the customer's bank (the issuer).
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What is merchant?">
    Customers that will use Payment Options as their payment gateway are merchants.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What is partner (reseller)?">
    Customers that onboard other merchants onto the Payment Options gateway services are partners.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What is API?">
    An API (Application Programming Interface) is a set of rules, protocols, and tools that allows different software applications to communicate and interact with each other. It defines the methods and data formats that applications can use to request and exchange information. For more information, [Click here](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/API).
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What is an issuer?">
    An issuer is a bank or financial institution that provides credit cards, debit cards, or other payment cards to consumers (cardholders). The issuer is responsible for approving the cardholder's application, managing their account, and making sure the funds are available for purchases or withdrawals.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What is Dasgateway?">
    Dasgateway is the name of our payment gateway platform. To access Dasgateway, please log in to your merchant or partner account [here](https://me.paymentoptions.com/login).
  </Accordion>
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Payment Processing">
  <Accordion title="What payment methods does the Gateway support?">
    Our Gateway supports multiple payment methods, including credit cards, e-wallets, QR codes, and more. Check the [Supported Card Schemes](./guides/card-schemes) and [E-Wallets](./guides/e-wallets) sections for details.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How do I enable 3D Secure (3DS) transactions?">
    3D Secure is enabled at the product level. Visit the **Secure Payment** section to understand how to configure 3DS for enhanced transaction security.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I process multi-currency payments?">
    Yes, our Gateway supports multiple currencies. For a list of supported currencies, refer to the [Currency Guide](./guides/currency).
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What is chargeback?">
    A chargeback is a process in which a credit card or debit card transaction is reversed by the cardholder's bank (the issuer) after a customer disputes a charge. Essentially, a chargeback allows the customer to get their money back if they believe a transaction was fraudulent, erroneous, or otherwise problematic.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What is refund?">
    A refund is the return of money to a customer after they have paid for goods or services, typically because the customer is dissatisfied with the purchase or because there was an issue with the product or service. A refund is a voluntary action by the merchant (or seller), unlike a chargeback, which is initiated by the customer through their bank.
  </Accordion>
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Gateway">
  <Accordion title="When will the statement come out?">
    Weekly Statements:
    Weekly statements cover the period from Monday to Sunday and are released on the following Monday of the next week.

    Monthly Statements:
    Monthly statements cover the period from the 1st to the end of the month and are released on the second Monday of the following month.
    Note: If the scheduled Monday falls on a holiday, statements will be released on the next business day.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I see transactions in real time?">
    Yes, you may see it in real time on your [Dasgateway](https://me.paymentoptions.com/login) page.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How do I add extra columns in the gateway?">
    On the Transaction page, press the "Additional column" button on the top left and select the fields you wish to be added.
  </Accordion>

    Please note: Once your account is terminated, all data associated with it may be permanently deleted, and you will no longer have access to any services associated with your account.

</Accordion>

## **Troubleshooting Guide**

This section addresses common issues users encounter. If you’re experiencing a specific problem, review the solutions below.

<Accordion title="Account Issues">
  <Accordion title="I forgot my password. How can I reset it?">
    Go to the login page and click **Forgot Password**. Follow the instructions to reset your password via email.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Why am I receiving a 'Login Failed' error?">
    Double-check your username and password. Ensure you’re using the correct credentials associated with your account. If the issue persists, contact support.
  </Accordion>
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Payment Errors">
  <Accordion title="What does the 'Insufficient Funds' error mean?">
    This error occurs when the cardholder's account has insufficient funds. Ask the customer to try another card or contact their bank.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How can I resolve a '3DS Authentication Failed' error?">
    Ensure the customer has completed the 3D Secure authentication step. For more on 3DS, see the [Secure Payment](./guides/secure-payment) page.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Transaction was declined without a clear reason. What should I do?">
    Review the [Gateway Error Codes](./guides/gateway-error-codes) page for information on decline codes. Contact support if the issue is unresolved.
  </Accordion>
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Technical Issues">
  <Accordion title="API request is returning a 401 Unauthorized error. What does this mean?">
    This error indicates incorrect API credentials. Verify the `Authorization` header in your request and ensure your API key is correctly formatted.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="My plugin installation failed. How can I fix this?">
    Ensure you’re using the correct plugin version for your e-commerce platform. Follow the instructions in the relevant [Installation Guide](./guides/website-plugins).
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Webhook events aren’t being received. What should I check?">
    Make sure the webhook URL is publicly accessible and registered correctly in the API payload. Refer to [Webhook Handler Setup](./guides/webhook-handler) for configuration steps.
  </Accordion>
</Accordion>
